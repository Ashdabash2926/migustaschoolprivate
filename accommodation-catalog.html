A house, a
courtyard, & four
kinds of rooms.

Casa Migusta sits a few cobbled blocks from Sucre's main square, in a colonial-era building we've slowly stripped back to its best self — whitewashed walls, original wooden beams, a courtyard that catches the sun in the morning and stays cool through the long afternoons. It's the home of our small Spanish school, and a place to actually sleep while you're studying with us.

We keep things simple on purpose. There are four kinds of rooms in the house — a sociable six-bed dorm, a snug private single, family rooms that fit a small group comfortably, and three private rooms with their own ensuite bathrooms. All of them share the same courtyard, the same kitchen, the same hot water, and the same morning light.

Whatever you book, you get real keys, a proper mattress, a desk to lay your notebooks on, and the kind of quiet you only find in old colonial walls. The pages that follow show each room in detail, with the rates we offer to our students.

The Six-bed Dorm
shared
— the rooms · in detail

A bunk, a locker,
and good morning light.

01of four

Our biggest shared room sleeps six in three sets of solid wooden bunks. Mattresses are proper — not the foam-and-prayer mattresses you'll find elsewhere — and each bed has its own reading light, a power outlet, and a curtain you can pull for a bit of privacy when the rest of the room is asleep.

There's a locker per person (bring a padlock or borrow one from us), a long shared desk against the window, and a chest of drawers in the corner. The bathroom is shared with the rest of the floor, with two showers and three WCs — there's never really a queue.

Sometimes you don't want a bunk and a roommate; sometimes you want the door shut, your books on a desk that's yours, and your own quiet corner of the house. The private single is exactly that — a small but well-made room with a proper single bed, a writing desk under the window, and a wardrobe big enough for whatever you packed.

The bathroom is shared with two other rooms on the same hallway, and is rarely busy. We keep the room warm in the cold months and bright in the warm ones, and the courtyard is two doors away when you want company again.

Our largest non-ensuite room is built for groups travelling together — parents with children, a small group of friends, anyone who wants to share a room without sharing a bunk. There's a proper double bed on one side, two singles tucked against the opposite wall, and a small sofa in the corner that doubles as a fifth seat at homework time.

Storage is generous: a wide wardrobe, a chest of drawers, and a luggage rack. The bathroom is shared with one other room on the floor — most weeks, that other room is empty or also a family, and the rhythm just works. Linens and towels for everyone are included.

Three of the rooms in the house come with a private ensuite bathroom — a rainfall shower with reliably hot water, proper towels, a basin tucked into a small wood vanity, and a door that locks behind you. Each room is slightly different, but all three sleep one or two: a queen bed, a desk under the window, and storage enough for a longer stay.

These are the rooms we recommend to anyone staying more than a fortnight, anyone travelling as a couple, and anyone who simply prefers their own bathroom. They are also the first to be booked, so we always recommend asking by WhatsApp before you settle on dates.
