A house, a
courtyard, & six
kinds of rooms.

Casa Migusta sits a few cobbled blocks from Sucre's main square, in a colonial-era building we've slowly stripped back to its best self — whitewashed walls, original wooden beams, a courtyard that catches the morning sun and stays cool through the long afternoons. It's the home of our small Spanish school, and a place to actually sleep while you're studying with us.

There are six rooms in the house. Three of them are private with their own en-suite bathrooms. One is a family room built for groups; another is a quieter private single; and the last is a sociable six-bed dorm. Above them all, two rooftop terraces hold the best light in Sucre at dusk.

Whatever you book, you get real keys, a proper mattress, a desk for your notebooks, and the quiet you only find in old colonial walls. The pages that follow show each room in detail, with the rates we offer to our students.

IIcapítulo dos

Three rooms in the house come with a private en-suite bathroom — a rainfall shower with reliably hot water, proper towels, a basin tucked into a small wood vanity, and a door that locks behind you. Each room is slightly different in shape and outlook, but all three sleep one or two: a queen bed, a desk under the window, and storage enough for a longer stay.

Room 1 is the bright one, with morning light through a tall window and a writing desk we've come to love. Room 2 is the quietest of the three, set deeper in the house. Room 3 catches the corner-light at the end of the afternoon. These are the rooms we recommend to anyone staying more than a fortnight, anyone travelling as a couple, and anyone who simply prefers their own bathroom — and they're the first to book.

Chapter V
The Six-bed Dorm · No. 6
shared —
— the rooms · in detail

A bunk, a locker,
and good morning light.

Vcapítulo cinco

Our biggest shared room sleeps six in three sets of solid wooden bunks. Mattresses are proper — not the foam-and-prayer mattresses you'll find elsewhere — and each bed has its own reading light, a power outlet, and a curtain you can pull for a bit of privacy when the rest of the room is asleep.

There's a locker per person (bring a padlock or borrow one from us), a small kitchenette with a hob and a sink, and a shared bathroom on the same floor with two basins, a WC and a hot rainfall shower. Linens and a towel are included.
