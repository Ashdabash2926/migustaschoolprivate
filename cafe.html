A café that feels like home

Me Gusta Café lives inside a beautifully preserved colonial building on Bolivar Street. Step through the arched doorway and you'll find a light-filled interior, an open courtyard wrapped in tropical plants, and the kind of unhurried atmosphere that makes one coffee turn into three.

Whether you're reviewing your Spanish notes between classes, catching up on work, or simply enjoying your morning — there's always a table waiting.

Colonial Architecture Original 19th-century building with high ceilings, archways, and a stunning central courtyard.
Free WiFi — Fast & Reliable A unique study space for all students and guests. Bring your laptop and settle in.
Open-Air Courtyard Dine under the open sky in our plant-filled colonial patio — one of Sucre's hidden gems.
from bean to cup

Coffee with a story

Every cup we serve is sourced from regions with extraordinary growing conditions — from the misty valleys of Bolivia to the fields of northern Italy. We want you to taste the difference.

01
Yungas, Bolivia

Yungas Valley

Grown in the cloud forests of the Bolivian Andes at 1,500–2,000m. Rich volcanic soil and high altitude produce a coffee of exceptional clarity and complexity.

Dark chocolate Citrus Floral
02
Caranavi, Bolivia

Caranavi Hills

Bolivia's most celebrated coffee-growing region. Fertile foothills and an ideal microclimate produce a naturally sweet, full-bodied cup that's become a world favourite.

Brown sugar Walnut Stone fruit
03
Northern Italy

Lavazza

Italy's most iconic coffee house, roasting since 1895. For those who want that classic, robust Italian espresso — smooth, strong, and completely reliable.

Roasted nuts Caramel Classic espresso

A unique study space unlike any other

Me Gusta Café is open to all students as a dedicated study and relaxation space. Between classes — or after — pull up a chair, order something delicious, connect to the WiFi, and let the language sink in.

Many students find that reviewing vocabulary over a cortado, or practising conversation over lunch, is where the real learning happens. The café is more than a café — it's part of the school.
